Car Crash Investigations

Since 1987, Vicroads have maintained a database of car crashes on Victorian roads called the Road Crash Information System, with the data sourced from Victoria Police.

Although access to the full RCIS database is restricted to government bodies, the Victorian Government Data Directory provides a publicly available extract from the database, which summarises the data over the past five years (licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International).

The dataset we have used comprises every recorded crash on Victorian roads which resulted in a fatility or injury, spanning the period 1 January 2012 to 10 May 2017. The dataset contains information such as the type and severity of the crash, date, time, location, numbers of people and vehicles involved, numbers of injuries and fatalities, and more.

Over the last 5 years, 73,491 crashes were recorded on Victorian roads.

Bokeh Plot

In this Bokeh Plot, each area represents one Victorian Local Government Area. The data visualized in this plot is the proportion of crashes that involve Young Drivers in any given Area. If you mouseover an area, you will find the Local Government Area name, the number of Young Drivers involved in crashes over the past 5 years, and the percentage of crashes in that Area that involved Young Drivers.

Using the "Box Zoom" tool (on the right-hand side toolbar) we could investigate the areas of Victoria where a crash is more likely to involve Young Driver. If we zoom in to Melbourne, we find that the highest rate of Young Driver crashes are in Casey and Hume.

There also appears to be a trend among the cities and towns in Victoria. Young Drivers are involved in a higher percentage of crashes in Areas such as Horsham, Ballarat, Geelong, Warrnambool, Bendigo and Wodonga in comparison to the areas surrounding them. This is expected; recall from the first page that Young Drivers are more likely to be involved in a collision with other Vehicles (and, of course, in towns the number of vehicles that you share the roads with increases).
